About My Projects

1. V2G-Sim at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboaratory.
Available on Github Repo.

This is a vehicle to grid simulator to predict/simulate Plug-In Electrical Vehicles (PEVs) impact on the grid. My role was developing the PHEV battery degradation model for this simulator. Paper published on Journal of Power Sources (See Publications).

2. PEV charging piles data scraping at eCAL (Energy, Control & Application Lab).
Available on Github Repo.

This is a mobile app simulator for connection with EV charging company and queries of the daily utilizaiton data. I am the major developer to this program.

3. PEV charging station planning
Paper on Arxiv. **Journal Paper Submitted to IEEE Trans. Industrial Informatics

This is a NSF STTR Phase I. project collaborating with colleagues from Microgrid Lab (MGL) . The project is specifically focusing on PEV charging station planning with PEV overstaying issue taken into account. Target customers are small business operator, who are interested in providing charging service to attract business. Eventually, as it moves on to Phase II, there will be more of charging controller development and business partnership development on the MGL side. UC Berkeley, as the research institute, is interested in researching about the technology.
